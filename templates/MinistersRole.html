1. Assessment of the Job Seeker s Needs

  • Understand their skills, experience, and career goals. [link to assessment sheet forth coming]
  • Identify any barriers to employment (e.g., lack of skills, gaps in work history, confidence issues).
  • Determine their immediate needs (e.g., financial assistance, transportation, childcare).

2. Emotional and Motivational Support

  • Check in regularly to offer encouragement and accountability.
  • Help them set realistic job search goals and timelines.
  • Provide a safe space to discuss frustrations and concerns.

3. Provide accountability

  • Determine level of support the person desires.
  • If appropriate set time for regular meetings. [link to agenda forth coming]
  • Set goals and task together and follow-up
  • Encourage them to fill out Roles Document [link forth coming] 

4. Follow-Up and Continuous Support

  • Offer ongoing assistance even after they secure a job to ensure long-term success.
  • Provide mentorship and career advice for growth opportunities.
  • Help them develop a plan for career advancement.

 

 

Things to consider

1. Offer Encouragement and Support

  • Listen Actively: Provide a safe space for the individual to share their concerns and struggles without judgment.
  • Pray with Them: Offer spiritual encouragement through prayer and affirmations of their worth and potential.
  • Boost Confidence: Remind them of their unique gifts and skills, reassuring them that they are valued.

2. Connect Them with Resources

  • Local Job Boards: Share information about job boards, career fairs, or hiring events in the area.
  • Community Networks: Use connections within the congregation or community to identify job opportunities.
  • Workshops and Seminars: Direct them to r sum -building workshops, interview preparation seminars, or online training courses.

3. Leverage Congregational Resources

  • Create a Job Support Group: Organize a support group for job seekers in the congregation to share resources, tips, and encouragement.
  • Skill-Sharing Opportunities: Encourage congregants with professional expertise (e.g., HR professionals) to offer guidance or mentorship.
  • Job Bulletin Board: Set up a space (physical or virtual) to post job leads shared by the congregation.

4. Assist Practically

  • Help with Resumes and Applications: Offer to review resumes, cover letters, and online profiles like LinkedIn.
  • Mock Interviews: Practice interview scenarios to help build their confidence and readiness.
  • Transportation or Childcare: Assist with practical barriers by organizing rides to interviews or helping with childcare.

5. Address Emotional and Spiritual Well-Being

  • Encourage Patience and Resilience: Share scriptures or stories that inspire perseverance, such as Philippians 4:13 or Proverbs 3:5-6.
  • Combat Shame or Guilt: Reassure them that periods of unemployment are part of life's challenges and not a reflection of their value.
  • Offer Counseling: If needed, provide pastoral counseling to help them cope with stress, anxiety, or discouragement.

6. Partner with Local Organizations

  • Collaborate with Nonprofits: Work with local charities or organizations that focus on workforce development.
  • Advocate for Assistance: Guide them toward government programs, food banks, or financial aid to ease their burden during the job search.
